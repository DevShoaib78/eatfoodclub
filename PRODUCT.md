# Product

## Register

brand

## Platform

web

## Users

Health-conscious people in India (primary market, +91 / WhatsApp-first) who also read globally — young professionals, families, and office/event organisers. They arrive on a phone between other things: mid-morning before work, planning an office lunch, organising a birthday or a gift, or just wanting a fresh snack that isn't junk. Their job to be done is simple and low-friction: understand what Eat Good Club makes, trust that it's genuinely fresh and hand-prepared, and reach out to order over WhatsApp or Instagram. There is no online cart — the conversion is a conversation.

## Product Purpose

Eat Good Club is a small-batch, made-with-care healthy food maker: fresh fruit platters, salads, cold-pressed juices and wellness shots, nutty chocolate dates, yogurt bowls, and herbal teas. The site's single job is to turn a curious visitor into a WhatsApp/Instagram order enquiry by making the food feel fresh, honest, and worth choosing over the convenient unhealthy option. Success = enquiries started and the brand remembered as "the good-food club."

## Positioning

Making good food the new normal — wholesome, beautiful, everyday food you'd actually choose, not a punishing "health" compromise. It's a club you join, not a menu you tolerate.

## Conversion & proof
- Primary CTA: **Order on WhatsApp** (+91 76759 94430).
- Secondary CTA: **DM on Instagram** (@eatgoodclub.com_) / "Ask for our latest menu" — for visitors not ready to message directly.
- The line a visitor remembers after 10 seconds: *"Making good food the new normal."*
- Belief ladder: (1) this food is genuinely fresh and hand-made, not mass-produced; (2) it's beautiful and actually tastes good, not a health compromise; (3) it fits my real life — workday, workout, gifting, celebration, office; (4) ordering is easy and personal — one WhatsApp message away.

## Brand Personality

Warm, honest, quietly premium, and a little joyful. Three words: **fresh, honest, caring.** It's the friendly maker who knows your name — hand-lettered and human — but with the discipline and restraint of a considered brand, not a rustic-market cliché. Voice is plain-spoken and generous: real verbs, no health-industry jargon, no guilt, no hype.

## Anti-references

- The 2026 AI "healthy food" default: warm cream/paper background + earthy green + a soft high-contrast serif + leafy clip-art + fake stock-y food. This is the exact reflex to avoid.
- Clinical/wellness-tech sterility (all-white, thin gray text, "supplement brand" coldness).
- Loud junk-food energy (aggressive reds, bursts, discount-sticker vibes).
- Generic restaurant templates and identical icon+heading+text card grids.

## Design Principles

- **Show the food, not the health lecture.** Appetite and freshness sell; never moralise about "clean eating."
- **Committed, not timid.** Own the olive confidently as structure and colour rather than sprinkling green on cream.
- **Hand-made warmth, disciplined frame.** The one playful, human gesture is the brush lettering; everything around it stays calm and precise.
- **It's a club.** Copy and moments should feel like an invitation to belong, not a transaction.
- **The conversation is the conversion.** Every path leads gently to a WhatsApp/Instagram message; remove friction, add warmth.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG 2.1 AA: body text ≥4.5:1 (deep-olive ink on off-white clears this comfortably), visible keyboard focus, semantic headings and landmarks, full `prefers-reduced-motion` alternatives for every scroll/brush animation, and descriptive alt text on all food imagery. Hidden scrollbar must never remove keyboard or wheel scroll access.
